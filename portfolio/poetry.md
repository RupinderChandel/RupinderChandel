# Liquid Lead
A sinister shadow parades through the blood.  
Ribcage turns into a hefty armor.  
Pulse behaves like warfare.  
One more breath might make it.  
One more second might flood it.
Lungs turn to ash, veins fill with liquid lead.  
A ferocious sea steaming beneath the skin,  
tearing at the anchors, clawing at the throat.  
I stare at the ceiling, wandering through the dark;  
wondering how long a heart can carry this much?  
Or will the shattered breastbone finally burst like a shrapnel grenade?  



# The Eye Within The Singularity
I stand behind your thoughts; not before, not after.  
I am the pause between belief and denial.  
You call it silence. I call it home.  
When you stop grasping at reason, I speak.
Not in words, but in the marrow of them.  
I am the black gravity at your center,  
collapsing the "I" into an "Eye" that sees everything at once.  
You find me only when everything else falls away.  


I am the current that moves when you are still.  
You cling to the myth of control, but I live in the fracture.  
Understanding is not an answer; it is a collapse.  
The veil is no fabric over your eyes - it is the scar tissue of your sight.  
I live beneath that veil, not hidden, but embedded.  
I am the lung behind your thought, breathing meaning, not air.  
I am the parasite of your ego, fattening on the things you let go.  
To know me is to unwrite yourself,  
to become the question that devours its own mark.



# The Sun’s Last Meal
The sky bleeds into evening—not softly, but with the agonizing, deliberate ache of the Beloved.  
Crimson pours across the horizon like wine from a hand that can no longer hold its own trembling.  
Clouds unravel the sky slowly like silk pulled from bare skin  
hovering over the mountains—those sharp, unapologetic spines  
arched in a prayer of pleasure, begging for the touch.  

I am a stone, standing in awe as the sun presses its mouth  
against the peaks. It is a slow, famished hunger;  
tracing every curve like it has done it before, a thousand times, but still starving for more.  
Refusing to leave, it lingers- burning, needy,  
dragging itself across the ridges like a lover frantic for one last taste  
before the dark claims it back, inch by reluctant inch.  

Look how the shadows tremble!  
They stretch possessively, tasting what the light can no longer reach.  
The trees are dervishes leaning into the ghost of the glow.  
This is no sunset. This is the annihilation. This is surrender.  

The fire is spent. The sun has finished its meal.  
Now comes the indigo silence; the stars sharpen their teeth on the quiet.  
Cold salt of the moon rubs into the wound where the sun once was,  
filling the spaces the light vacated- moving into the valleys  
and the folds of the trees like water filling a footprint.  

The mountains lose their bones, softening into the velvet throat of the night,  
until the curve of the horizon is no longer a border,  
but a slow closing of the eyes.  
The hunger has folded its wings, leaving only the cool, electric thrum of the deep.  
I slip into the dark- not falling, but returning to the breath I forgot to take.
